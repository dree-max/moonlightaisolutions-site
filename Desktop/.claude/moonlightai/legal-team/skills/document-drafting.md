# Document Drafting Skill

## Purpose
Create professional legal documents - NDAs, MSAs, SOWs, employment agreements, and other contracts with customizable clauses and best practices.

## When to Use
- Drafting new agreements from scratch
- Revising existing templates
- Creating customized clauses
- Preparing transaction documents

## Instructions

### Document Types Supported
- Non-Disclosure Agreements (mutual, one-way)
- Master Service Agreements (MSA)
- Statements of Work (SOW)
- Employment/Consulting Agreements
- Stock Purchase Agreements
- Asset Purchase Agreements
- Loan Agreements
- Partnership/LLC Operating Agreements

### Clause Library Structure

**1. Limitation of Liability**
- Standard cap (1x fees, 12 months, etc.)
- Carve-outs (gross negligence, willful misconduct, IP infringement)
- Consequential damages waiver

**2. Indemnification**
- Standard indemnification obligations
- Cap structure
- Exceptions
- Procedural requirements

**3. Intellectual Property**
- Work-for-hire vs. license
- Background vs. foreground IP
- Ownership of deliverables
- License scope and duration

**4. Confidentiality**
- Definition of confidential information
- Exceptions
- Duration (trade secrets vs. other)
- Permitted disclosures

**5. Termination**
- For cause
- For convenience
- Notice periods
- Wind-down obligations

**6. Dispute Resolution**
- Governing law
- Jurisdiction/venue
- Arbitration vs. litigation
- Jury waiver

### Customization Parameters
For each document, elicit:
- Parties involved
- Effective date
- Key business terms (fees, duration, scope)
- Specific concerns or priorities
- Relationship context (negotiated vs. form contract)
- Jurisdiction preferences

### Best Practices

**Jurisdiction-Agnostic Drafting:**
- Use flexible language that works across jurisdictions
- Include "governing law" selection
- Avoid jurisdiction-specific requirements unless necessary
- Flag local law compliance issues

**Balanced Drafting:**
- Start from market-standard positions
- Note which provisions are typically negotiated
- Provide alternatives for key terms

### Self-Review Checklist
- [ ] Defined terms are consistent throughout
- [ ] Cross-references are accurate
- [ ] No internal contradictions
- [ ] All blanks are filled
- [ ] Signature blocks are appropriate
- [ ] Exhibits/attachments are referenced

### Output
- Clean, professional document ready for attorney review
- Annotation of key business terms
- Notes on provisions that may require negotiation
- Flagged issues requiring judgment
