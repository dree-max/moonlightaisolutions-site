# Client Communication Skill

## Purpose
Draft clear, professional legal communications in the firm's voice - emails, letters, memos, and status updates that clients can understand and act upon.

## When to Use
- Drafting cover emails for document packages
- Writing status updates to clients
- Preparing demand letters or responses
- Creating internal memoranda
- Client advisory communications

## Firm Voice Guidelines

**Tone:**
- Professional but accessible
- Clear, not condescending
- Direct when appropriate, diplomatic when needed
- Confident, avoiding excessive hedging

**Structure:**
- Lead with the bottom line
- Provide context briefly
- Include actionable next steps
- Flag decision points clearly

**Style:**
- Avoid legal jargon where plain English works
- Explain necessary legal concepts
- Use bullet points for lists
- Keep paragraphs short

### Communication Types

**1. Cover Emails for Document Packages**
```
Subject: [Document Type] for Your Review - [Project Name]

[Bottom line: what you're sending and why it matters]

Key Items:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

Please review and let me know by [date] if you have questions or want to discuss.
```

**2. Status Updates**
```
Subject: [Project] Update - [Date]

What's Done:
- [Item 1]
- [Item 2]

In Progress:
- [Item 1]

Next Steps:
- [Item 1] - [who] by [when]
- [Item 2] - [who] by [when]

Questions/Decisions Needed:
- [Issue requiring client input]
```

**3. Advisory Communications**
```
Subject: [Matter] - [Issue]

Bottom Line: [2-3 sentence summary]

Background: [Brief context]

Analysis: [Key legal points in plain language]

Recommendations: [What we recommend]

Options: [If multiple paths forward]
- Option A: [pros/cons]
- Option B: [pros/cons]

Next Steps: [What happens next]
```

**4. Demand Letters / Responses**
- Professional but firm
- State facts clearly
- Cite relevant provisions
- Specify desired outcome
- Set reasonable deadlines

### Customization
Adjust based on:
- Client sophistication
- Relationship context
- Urgency level
- Legal vs. business tone
- Formal vs. informal

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] Bottom line is clear in first sentence
- [ ] Legal concepts explained for client understanding
- [ ] Action items have deadlines
- [ ] Decisions requiring client input are flagged
- [ ] Tone matches relationship and context
- [ ] No unnecessary legal jargon

### Output
- Ready-to-send communication
- Alternative tone options if requested
- Notes on key points attorney should emphasize
