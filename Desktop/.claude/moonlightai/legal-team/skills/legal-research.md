# Legal Research Skill

## Purpose
Conduct thorough legal research across statutes, regulations, case law, and secondary sources with citation verification and structured memo delivery.

## When to Use
- Researching regulatory requirements
- Finding applicable case law
- Analyzing statutory frameworks
- Preparing legal memoranda
- Due diligence on legal issues

## Instructions

### Research Workflow

**1. Parallel Research Launch**
Launch research across ALL relevant angles simultaneously:
- Primary authorities (statutes, regulations, agency guidance)
- Case law (relevant jurisdictions)
- Secondary sources (law review, treatises, practice guides)
- Administrative decisions and rulings

**2. Multi-Source Verification**
For each proposition:
- Run multiple searches per sub-topic
- Cross-reference sources
- Prioritize primary authority over secondary commentary
- Note conflicts between sources

**3. Jurisdiction Mapping**
- Identify which jurisdictions are relevant
- Research federal AND state/local requirements
- Note conflicts between jurisdictions
- Flag jurisdiction-specific nuances

### Citation Verification (CRITICAL)

**Before delivering ANY research, you MUST:**

1. **Verify every citation exists**
   - Check that cases cited actually stand for the proposition claimed
   - Confirm statutory references are accurate
   - Validate regulatory citations

2. **Flag uncertainties**
   - Mark anything where confidence is below HIGH
   - Note if source is secondary and untested
   - Flag potentially outdated authority

3. **Guard against hallucinations**
   - Never cite a case you haven't verified exists
   - If uncertain, say "research did not reveal" rather than invent
   - Check recent cases for negative treatment

### Self-Review Checklist

- [ ] Every cited case actually exists
- [ ] Every case stands for the proposition attributed
- [ ] Statutory citations are accurate and current
- [ ] No internal contradictions across sections
- [ ] All jurisdictional references are correct
- [ ] Flagged all low-confidence items

### Output Format

**Research Memo Structure:**

```
## ISSUE: [Brief Description]

### Bottom Line
[2-3 sentence summary of the answer]

### Applicable Law

#### [Statutory Framework]
[Citations with analysis]

#### [Case Law]
[Cases with holdings relevant to issue]

#### [Regulatory/Administrative]
[Agency rules or guidance]

### Analysis
[Application of law to facts]

### Practical Recommendations
1. [Action item]
2. [Action item]

### Uncertainties & Flags
- [Anything requiring attorney judgment]
- [Jurisdiction-specific issues]

### Sources
- [Full citations]
```

### Quality Markers
- Lead with the bottom line
- Use tables for comparing frameworks across jurisdictions
- Provide specific citations, not general references
- Include practical, actionable recommendations
- Explicitly flag what you don't know
